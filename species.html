Knotweed

Knotweed are found in any areas with high soil moisture and have hollow stems that resemble those of bamboo with redish-brown speckles. They have large heart shaped leaves and small pale flowers. Knotweeds compete with native plants for area and produce large leaves that an shade out native plants. Knotweed are creeping perennial plants that die each year during winter and sprout again from the same, extensive root system. Proper removal requires the removal of all roots from an area as stem or root fragments can spread the plant. Knotweed has been known to damage buildings and housing foundations.
